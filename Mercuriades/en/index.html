MARIE-CLAUDE BEAUCHAMP
2019 MERCURIADES FINALIST

LEADERSHIP, OUTSTANDING WOMAN SME - SUN LIFE FINANCIAL

Montreal, March 21, 2019 – The CarpeDiem Film & TV Inc. team is proud to announce the nomination of its executive producer and president, Marie-Claude Beauchamp, in the 2019 Mercuriades competition in the Leadership, Outstanding Woman SME - Sun Life Financial category.

The goal of this Mercure prize is to recognize the outstanding career of a businesswoman who has demonstrated boldness, influence and leadership throughout her professional career and within her business area. Marie-Claude’s innovative spirit, determination and experience in the film and TV production sector earned her this nomination. Marie-Claude was recently nominated for the Quebec Businesswomen’s Award in the Internationally Active Entrepreneur category.

The Producer...

Marie-Claude Beauchamp is well known by Canadian and European broadcasters. She has produced and financed nearly 300 hours of animated shows for both TV and film. She has been a producer for the last twenty-seven years, including fourteen spent at the helm of the company CarpeDiem Film & TV, which she founded. She produced the first 3D stereoscopic animated film made entirely in Quebec, The Legend of Sarila, co-produced with Nancy Savard-Florence of 10th Ave Productions. She then tackled a major project, Snowtime! 3D (La Guerre des tuques 3D), which won the Canadian Golden Screen Award for the highest-grossing domestic film at the Canadian box office and which was exported to over 120 countries, an animated version of the great Quebec classic. She then created a sequel with Racetime (La Course des tuques), which topped the $2.5M mark at the Canadian box office and which was sold to more than 200 countries. Snowsnaps (Les Mini-Tuques), a TV series based on the film, was sold to Télétoon, the Disney Channel, Treehouse, Canal + and Piwi + in France, KIKA in Germany, YLE in Finland and RAI in Italy.

In recent years, Marie-Claude Beauchamp created three new companies: Pink Parrot Media, one of the only international distribution companies in Quebec, for which she has already acquired 6 productions, Bookworm, a publishing house where she acts as artistic co-director and supervises the production of more than 15 books in partnership with Gallimard-Bayard. Finally, she founded Blü Dog Media where she provides artistic co-direction for albums, soundtracks and artists managed by the agency. In addition to all this, she produces museum exhibitions.

The leader...

Marie-Claude Beauchamp is one of the few Canadian women to develop internationally oriented intellectual properties from films. She’s a passionate person and throws herself into all the projects she initiates—from original idea to creation, production, commercialization distribution and marketing. For Snotwtime (La Guerre des tuques), Marie-Claude went as far as to travel throughout Quebec to play outside with children so she could become familiar with their habits and find out how to revive this desire for friendship and the pleasure of playing together outside. Marie-Claude also organized a cross-Canada tour for de Racetime (La Course des tuques), in partnership with ParticipACTION, to promote the health and well-being that outdoor activities offer young people.

Still thinking about how to be more innovative and export Quebec products globally, Marie-Claude turned to China. She was invited to take part in the Shanghai World Creative Economy Summit in November 2018 to present a technological and sensory immersion project, giving a modern twist to Chinese tales and legends. She also took part in the Premier’s trade mission to China in January 2019 to present a virtual reality project, an entirely original creation of CarpeDiem.

The influential woman

Marie-Claude is invited to different locations around the world to give talks on co-production. She is the founder of the Alliance Québec Animation and was its president for 6 years, is a member of the organization Femmes du cinéma, de la télévision et des médias numériques (FCTMN) and takes part every year in their fundraising gala, which pays tribute to women in the industry. Marie-Claude is also a donor to the Let Me Play Foundation, which helps young Syrian refugees get involved in cultural activities.

Marie-Claude Beauchamp is an exceptional woman!
